Our Research & Innovation

At Xschizq, we are at the forefront of applying cutting-edge Artificial Intelligence to transform precision psychiatry. Our research focuses on leveraging advanced computational methods and diverse data modalities to predict and optimize treatment outcomes for mental health disorders.

AI/ML Methodologies

We utilize a range of powerful AI and Machine Learning algorithms, including **Support Vector Machines (SVM)**, **Random Forests (RF)**, **Gradient Boosting Machines (GBM)**, and **Deep Learning (DL) models** like Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) and Recurrent Neural Networks (RNNs). These methods are crucial for processing complex, high-dimensional datasets and uncovering subtle patterns indicative of treatment response.

Multimodal Data Integration

Our approach integrates diverse data sources to create a comprehensive "digital twin" of each patient, enabling a holistic understanding of their unique profile:

  • Clinical Data: Electronic Health Records (EHRs), symptom severity scales, medication history, and clinical notes (analyzed with NLP).
  • Genetic Data: Pharmacogenomic markers and genetic predispositions influencing drug metabolism and response.
  • Neuroimaging Data: Insights from MRI, PET, fMRI, and especially **EEG-based biomarkers**, which show high predictive utility for treatment response.
  • Digital Phenotyping: Continuous, real-time behavioral and physiological data collected passively from smartphones, providing insights into mood, activity, and sleep patterns.

Specific Applications & Predictive Biomarkers

Our research specifically applies these methods to predict efficacy for D2 receptor partial agonist antipsychotics such as **Aripiprazole, Brexpiprazole, and Cariprazine**. We identify critical predictive biomarkers, including **gamma power in EEG for non-response in bipolar disorder** and **extracellular vesicle proteomics for schizophrenia diagnosis and antipsychotic response**, moving beyond traditional trial-and-error.

Accelerating Novel Drug Discovery

AI plays a transformative role in accelerating the entire drug discovery and development lifecycle. We utilize AI-enabled platforms for *de novo* drug design, identifying novel therapeutic targets (e.g., TAAR1 agonists like Ulotaront, M4 selective agonists like NBI-1117568), optimizing dose regimens, and designing more effective combination therapies. This significantly reduces the time and cost associated with bringing new treatments to patients.
